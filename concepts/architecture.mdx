Velixar isn't a vector database with an API. It's a **cognitive architecture** — a multi-layered system that stores, organizes, connects, encrypts, and evolves knowledge over time.

## How Recall Works

When you search for a memory, Velixar does more than vector similarity:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Semantic Search">
    Your query is embedded and matched against encrypted vectors. Top candidates are decrypted and ranked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Knowledge Graph Traversal">
    Related nodes in the knowledge graph are pulled in — if you recall "user's tech stack", connected facts about their database, framework, and deployment target come along.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Salience Boost">
    Recalled memories automatically increase in salience. Frequently accessed knowledge rises to the top. Dormant memories stay available but don't crowd active context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Audit Logging">
    The recall event is recorded in the audit trail — what was recalled, when, and in what context.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Infinite Memory

Velixar never deletes memories. There are no TTLs, no decay, no pruning. Every memory stored is available forever.

Instead of deletion, Velixar uses **salience** — a dynamic score that determines how prominently a memory surfaces in search results. Active memories have high salience. Dormant memories have low salience but can be recalled at any time, and when they are, their salience climbs back up.

This means your AI system builds a genuine long-term memory that grows more valuable over time.

## Workspace Isolation

Every workspace operates in complete isolation:

- Separate encryption keys per workspace
- Separate vector collections (optional)
- Separate knowledge graph namespace
- Separate audit trail
- No cross-workspace data leakage, ever

Org-tier memories can be explicitly promoted from a workspace to the organization level, with full lineage tracking.
